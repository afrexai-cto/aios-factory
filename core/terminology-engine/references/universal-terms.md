# Universal Terms Reference

Detailed reference for all 10 universal terms used by the terminology engine.

---

## Term 1: Case

**Core meaning**: The primary unit of work the system processes. Everything else orbits around a case.

**Characteristics:**
- Has a lifecycle (created, active, closed)
- Has an owner (the provider)
- Belongs to a client
- Produces an outcome
- May generate an invoice

**How to identify the case in a new vertical:**
Ask: "What is the fundamental unit of work this business does?" The answer is the case.
- A law firm processes matters
- A dental practice sees appointments
- A landscaping company completes jobs
- An agency delivers projects

**Edge cases:**
- Nested cases: Some verticals have parent-child relationships (a project contains jobs). Both are cases at different levels. Use a parent reference to link them.
- Intake-as-case: Some verticals say "we opened a case." In the Five Elements, the intake creates the case — they are related but distinct events.

---

## Term 2: Client

**Core meaning**: The person or organization that submits work and receives the outcome.

**Characteristics:**
- External to the provider's organization
- Has contact information
- Owns one or more cases
- Receives invoices and reports

**How to identify the client in a new vertical:**
Ask: "Who pays for this work?" and "Who receives the result?" Usually the same person.

**Edge cases:**
- Two-sided markets: Recruitment has employers (clients) and candidates (beneficiaries). The client is whoever pays and instructs.
- Internal clients: Some businesses have internal work orders. Use a type field to distinguish internal from external clients.

---

## Term 3: Document

**Core meaning**: Any file, record, or structured data object associated with work.

**Characteristics:**
- Attached to a case or client
- Has a type (contract, report, photo, form)
- May have version history
- May have access controls

**How to identify documents in a new vertical:**
Ask: "What files or records move through this business?" Common answers: contracts, photos, forms, certificates, drawings, reports.

---

## Term 4: Workflow

**Core meaning**: The sequence of steps and states that work moves through.

**Characteristics:**
- Defined at the product level, not per case
- Can have multiple branches
- Determines which statuses are available
- Rarely surfaced to end users directly

**How to identify the workflow:**
Ask: "What steps does a piece of work go through from start to finish?" Map each step as a workflow stage.

---

## Term 5: Intake

**Core meaning**: The act and record of receiving new work into the system.

**Characteristics:**
- Creates the case entity
- Captures initial data
- May involve validation
- Produces a reference or confirmation

**How to identify intake in a new vertical:**
Ask: "How does new work arrive?" The answer describes the intake method. The act of receiving and recording it is the intake.

---

## Term 6: Outcome

**Core meaning**: The final result produced for a case.

**Characteristics:**
- Has a type (advice, completion, delivery, resolution)
- Is immutable once finalized
- Determines whether the case is complete
- May trigger invoicing

**How to identify the outcome:**
Ask: "What does the client consider 'done'?" The answer is the outcome.

---

## Term 7: Provider

**Core meaning**: The professional within the organization who performs the work.

**Characteristics:**
- Internal to the deploying organization
- Has a role or specialization
- Is assigned to cases
- Different from the client

**How to identify the provider:**
Ask: "Who does the actual work?" The answer is the provider. In small businesses, this is often the owner themselves.

---

## Term 8: Status

**Core meaning**: The current state of a case within its workflow.

**Universal statuses (always available):**

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Pending | Received but not started |
| In Progress | Being worked on |
| On Hold | Paused, waiting for something |
| Complete | Outcome delivered |
| Cancelled | Stopped before completion |

Verticals may add domain-specific statuses. For example, a conveyancing firm might add "Exchanged" and "Completed" as distinct statuses.

---

## Term 9: Report

**Core meaning**: A structured document generated by the system and delivered to the client or provider.

**Different from document**: Documents are inputs (uploaded by clients or received from third parties). Reports are outputs (generated by the system).

**How to identify reports in a new vertical:**
Ask: "What formal documents does this business produce for its clients?" The answer describes the reports.

---

## Term 10: Invoice

**Core meaning**: A financial record documenting what is owed for services.

**Note**: Many workspaces integrate with existing invoicing tools (Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe) rather than building their own. The invoice term still exists in the terminology but may map to an external system reference.

**How to identify the invoice term:**
Ask: "How does this business charge for its work?" The answer reveals the billing model and the term they use for the financial record.
